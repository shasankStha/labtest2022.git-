Nag Panchami

The Nag is worshipped as the god of rain and water and is believed to bestow his devotees with wealth and prosperity with his presence in the house. It is believed that pasting the picture of the snake god also safeguards the family from snakes and scorpions, and also from fire and lightning.

Special pujas are being observed in Nagpokhari and Taudaha in Kathmandu, Siddhapokhari in Bhaktapur and other ponds and shrines during this day.
